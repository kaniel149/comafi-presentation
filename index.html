Solar Financing Models — Global Reference

04
⚡

PPA — Power Purchase Agreement

  • Investor installs and operates the system
  • Client buys electricity at a fixed rate
  • No upfront investment from client
  • 15-25 year contracts
  • Most used model in C&I globally
📋

Solar Leasing

  • Fixed monthly payment
  • Ownership stays with financier
  • Maintenance included
  • Purchase option at term end
  • Ideal for SMEs
🏦

Bank Loan

  • Client owns the asset
  • Full tax benefits
  • Term: 5-10 years
  • Collateral: the system + savings
  • Highest long-term ROI

Other International Models

  • PACE (C-PACE): Repayment via property taxes
  • Solar Crowdfunding: Retail investors
  • Green Bonds: Corporate green debt
  • On-Bill Financing: Repayment on electricity bill
  • Shared Savings: Energy savings split

Financial Metrics — DSCR & Viability

05
DEBT SERVICE COVERAGE RATIO (DSCR)

What is DSCR?

DSCR = Net Operating Income / Debt Service

  • DSCR > 1.0 — Project generates enough to cover debt
  • DSCR 1.2-1.5 — Typical range required by banks
  • DSCR > 1.5 — Low-risk project

Example: 100 kWp C&I System

System cost$120,000 - $150,000
Annual generation~160,000 kWh
Annual savings (tariff)$24,000 - $32,000
Annual debt service~$18,000 (7 years)
Estimated DSCR1.3 - 1.8x
Payback4-6 years
RISK STRUCTURE

Natural Guarantees of Solar

  • Physical asset on client's roof
  • Panel warranty: 25-30 years
  • Inverter warranty: 10-15 years
  • Predictable output (measured solar irradiation)
  • PPA contract (power purchase agreement)
  • Equipment insurance

Risk Mitigation

  • Credit risk: PPA binds the consumer
  • Technical risk: 24/7 remote monitoring (Solar OS)
  • Production risk: Performance guarantee
  • FX risk: Costs in USD, revenues indexed to rising tariffs
  • Regulatory risk: Law 27.191 + FODIS = stable framework
